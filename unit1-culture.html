Culture & Characters

Understanding Chinese characters through structure, meaning, and culture

Unit 1: Chinese Names

Chinese characters often combine meaningful parts called radicals. Understanding them can help you guess meanings and remember characters more easily.

Character Stories

姓
Structure: 女 + 生

The radical 女 relates to women. In ancient China, some early clans were connected with maternal family lines. Later, 姓 came to mean “family name” or surname.

名
Structure: 夕 + 口

The character 名 originally suggested calling someone’s name at night. Today it simply means “name”.

人
Basic character

人 means “person”. Many characters related to people use the radical 亻, which is a side form of 人.

Radical Explorer

女
nǚ · woman / female

The radical 女 often appears in characters related to women, family, or traditional social roles.

亻
rén · person

The radical 亻 is a side form of 人 and often appears in characters related to people.

日
rì · sun / time

The radical 日 often appears in characters related to the sun, light, or time.
