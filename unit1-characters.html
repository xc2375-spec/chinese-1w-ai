Understanding Chinese characters

Unit 1 Characters

Chinese characters often combine meaningful parts called radicals. Understanding them can help you guess meanings and remember characters.

Character Stories

姓
Structure: 女 + 生

The radical 女 relates to women. In ancient China, some clans used maternal family names. Later, 姓 came to mean “family name” or surname.

名
Structure: 夕 + 口

The character 名 originally suggested calling someone's name at night. Today it simply means “name”.

人
Basic character

人 means “person”. Many characters related to people use the radical 亻.
